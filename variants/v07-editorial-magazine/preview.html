§ 01 Research Agenda Folio 03 — 06

The AURA Lab advances research at the intersection of efficiency, automation, and explainability in software engineering. Our mission: design innovative and responsible AI-driven solutions that improve developer productivity while addressing the key challenges of transparency and resource efficiency.

Through rigorous methods, open collaboration, and a commitment to impactful work, the lab contributes practical advances in AI-supported software engineering for academia, industry, and society. Four primary projects and one supporting direction define the current programme.

Est. · Fall 2024
Dept. · Computer Science, William & Mary
Founder · Dr. Antonio Mastropaolo
  1. 01

    Explainability & Interpretability

    Methods that reveal how code models reason — rationales, traces, and tests — so developers can inspect, trust, and debug automation rather than accept it on faith.

  2. 02

    Efficiency & Resource-Aware Automation

    Quantization, distillation, and PEFT techniques delivering low-latency, memory-light, cost-aware automation, measured on real workloads with reproducible metrics.

  3. 03

    Multi-Agent LLMs for SE

    Role-aligned agents that plan, code, test, and review — coordinated to produce coherent, end-to-end developer assistance rather than isolated suggestions.

  4. 04

    Neurosymbolic AI for SE

    Hybrid pipelines pairing LLMs with program analysis and constraints to produce interpretable, checkable fixes, tests, and designs.

  5. 05

    Task-Aware Code Automation

    From issues, diffs, and traces to concise summaries, TODOs, and plans — models that reduce developer toil with actionable, repo-aware guidance.

Innovative and responsible AI-driven solutions that improve developer productivity — while addressing the key challenges of transparency and resource efficiency.
