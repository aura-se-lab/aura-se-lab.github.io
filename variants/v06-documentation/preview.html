Overview

The AURA Lab · William & Mary, Department of Computer Science · Founded Fall 2024

The AURA Lab advances research at the intersection of efficiency, automation, and explainability in software engineering. Our mission is to design innovative and responsible AI-driven solutions that improve developer productivity while addressing key challenges of transparency and resource efficiency. Through rigorous methods, open collaboration, and a commitment to impactful work, the AURA Lab contributes practical advances in AI-supported software engineering for academia, industry, and society.

Acronym

AURA — AI for Understandable and Responsible Automation in Software Engineering. Directed by Dr. Antonio Mastropaolo, Assistant Professor at William & Mary.

Research avenues #

Four primary directions and one supporting direction. Each item links to representative work below.

  1. 01
    Explainability & Interpretability

    Methods that reveal how code models reason — rationales, traces, tests — so developers can inspect, trust, and debug automation.

  2. 02
    Efficiency & Resource-Aware Automation

    Quantization, distillation, and PEFT to deliver low-latency, memory-light, cost-aware automation measured on real workloads with reproducible metrics.

  3. 03
    Multi-Agent LLMs for SE

    Role-aligned agents that plan, code, test, and review — coordinated to produce coherent, end-to-end developer assistance.

  4. 04
    Neurosymbolic AI for SE

    Hybrid pipelines pairing LLMs with program analysis and constraints to produce interpretable, checkable fixes, tests, and designs.

  5. 05
    Task-Aware Code Automation supporting

    From issues, diffs, and traces to concise summaries, TODOs, and plans; models that reduce developer toil with actionable, repo-aware guidance.
