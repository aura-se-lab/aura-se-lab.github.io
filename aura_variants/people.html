§ 1 · Director

The principal investigator.

Antonio Mastropaolo
Assistant Professor · Director · Principal Investigator

Antonio Mastropaolo is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at William & Mary, where he directs the AURA Lab. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Università della Svizzera Italiana (USI Lugano) in 2024, his M.Sc. and B.Sc. from the University of Molise. His research lives at the intersection of AI, NLP, and software engineering — with an emphasis on explainability, efficiency, and trustworthiness.

He is the sole PI of an NSF CRII award (#2451058, 2025–2027) on trustworthy and resource-efficient foundation models for SE, and advises seven Ph.D. students at W&M.

§ 4 · We are hiring

Want to join?

Two PhD slots open for Fall 2026.

We look for: genuine interest in deep-learning systems or programming-language semantics (ideally both); solid programming (Python, plus willingness to read C/C++/Java/Go); a taste for empirical work — shipping reproducible experiments and writing carefully; curiosity about why a model behaves the way it does.

Undergraduate research opportunities at W&M are also available year-round. Get in touch directly.
